# 👨‍💼 Employee Attrition Analysis using Excel

---

## 📌 Executive Summary  
Employee attrition is a critical challenge for organizations, impacting productivity, cost, and workforce stability.  

This project analyzes employee data using **Microsoft Excel** to identify key drivers of attrition and provide actionable insights to improve **employee retention and organizational efficiency**.

---

## 🎯 Project Objective  
The primary objectives of this project are:

- Analyze employee attrition trends across different dimensions  
- Identify key factors influencing employee turnover  
- Evaluate the impact of salary, job satisfaction, and experience  
- Provide data-driven recommendations to improve retention strategies  

---


## ❓ Business Questions  
The project aims to answer the following critical questions:

- Which departments and roles experience the highest attrition?  
- How does compensation influence employee retention?  
- What is the relationship between job satisfaction and attrition?  
- Which employee segments are at the highest risk of leaving?  

---

## 📊 Dataset Description  

The dataset contains employee-level information with the following categories:

### 🔹 Demographics  
- Age  
- Gender  
- Education  

### 🔹 Job Information  
- Department  
- Job Role  

### 🔹 Financial Metrics  
- Monthly Income / Salary  

### 🔹 Experience Metrics  
- Years at Company  
- Total Working Experience  

### 🔹 Satisfaction Metrics  
- Job Satisfaction Levels  

### 🔹 Target Variable  
- Attrition (Yes/No)  

---

## 🧹 Data Cleaning & Preparation  

Data preprocessing steps performed:

- Handled missing and inconsistent values  
- Removed duplicate records  
- Standardized data formats  
- Created calculated fields for better analysis  

This ensured the dataset was accurate and suitable for analysis.

---

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies  

- Microsoft Excel  
- Pivot Tables  
- Pivot Charts  
- Slicers & Filters  

---

## 📏 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)  

The following KPIs were calculated to evaluate attrition:

- **Total Employees** – Overall workforce size  
- **Attrition Count** – Number of employees who left  
- **Attrition Rate (%)** – Percentage of employees leaving  
- **Average Salary** – Average compensation level  
- **Average Tenure** – Employee experience within the company  

---

## 📈 Analytical Approach  

The analysis was conducted using Excel pivot tables and charts across multiple dimensions:

---

### 🔹 1. Overall Attrition Analysis  
- Calculated total workforce and attrition rate  
- Provided a high-level overview of employee turnover  

---

### 🔹 2. Attrition by Department & Role  
- Used **bar charts** to compare attrition across departments and job roles  
- Identified high-risk departments with elevated turnover  

---

### 🔹 3. Attrition by Salary & Experience  
- Used **column charts** to analyze attrition by salary and tenure  
- Observed higher attrition among lower salary bands and early-stage employees  

---

### 🔹 4. Attrition by Job Satisfaction  
- Used **donut/pie charts** to evaluate satisfaction levels  
- Identified strong correlation between low satisfaction and higher attrition  

---

### 🔹 5. Interactive Analysis  
- Implemented **slicers and filters** for dynamic exploration  
- Enabled users to analyze attrition by department, role, and experience  

---

## 🔍 Key Insights  

- Lower salary levels are strongly associated with higher attrition  
- Employees in early career stages show higher turnover rates  
- Job satisfaction has a direct impact on employee retention  
- Certain departments consistently experience higher attrition  

---

## 💡 Business Recommendations  

Based on the analysis, the following strategies are recommended:

- 💰 Improve compensation structures for lower salary segments  
- 🚀 Strengthen onboarding and early employee engagement  
- 😊 Enhance job satisfaction through employee-focused initiatives  
- 🎯 Implement targeted retention strategies for high-risk departments  

---
## 📌 Conclusion  

This analysis provides valuable insights into the underlying factors driving employee attrition and offers actionable strategies to address them.  

By leveraging these insights, organizations can reduce turnover, optimize hiring costs, and enhance employee satisfaction, ultimately leading to improved organizational performance.  

The project demonstrates how data-driven decision-making can create measurable business impact in workforce management.
